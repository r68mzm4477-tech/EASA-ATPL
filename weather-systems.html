Weather Systems & Fronts

Cold Front

Steep frontal slope with rapid uplift. Typically associated with cumuliform cloud, turbulence, heavy showers and thunderstorms.

Warm Front

Gentle frontal slope producing layered cloud, widespread precipitation and deteriorating visibility ahead of the front.

Occluded Front

Forms when a cold front overtakes a warm front, combining characteristics of both systems.

Embedded CB Warning

Embedded cumulonimbus clouds may be hidden inside larger cloud layers and are particularly hazardous because they are difficult to identify visually.
